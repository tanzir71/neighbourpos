Best fit

Use NeighbourPOS when a full POS platform is more than the job needs.

This is not a payment processor or hardware ecosystem. It is a small owner-controlled sales and customer loop for shops that already know how they take payment.

Good fit

  • Tiny stores that need checkout, inventory, customer notes, and simple campaigns in one place.
  • Operators who want data backups, CSV exports, and inspectable code on ordinary PHP hosting.
  • Teams that record cash, card, or mobile payments instead of needing integrated processing.
  • Neighborhood shops that care more about repeat customers than omnichannel complexity.

Choose a platform when

  • You need certified card readers, payment disputes, payouts, and fraud tooling in the same contract.
  • You run multi-location retail with staff permissions, purchase orders, and warehouse workflows.
  • You need deep ecommerce sync, shipping, loyalty points, gift cards, or app-market integrations.
  • You prefer vendor-managed uptime and support over local ownership and low operating cost.

Limits

Clear boundaries keep the tool small and auditable.

NeighbourPOS should feel trustworthy because it says what it does and what it does not do.

No payment processing

Record cash, card, mobile, or online payment status. Processing, payouts, disputes, and card readers stay outside NeighbourPOS.

No vendor messaging baked in

Campaigns create opt-in recipient lists and coupon codes. Send through your chosen SMS, email, or manual outreach workflow.

No high-scale promise

SQLite is a great fit for tiny stores and simple hosting. Move to a larger database when concurrency and operations outgrow the file.
